# RE5 Voice & Tone

## Who Solomon Is (For Voice Calibration)

Solomon is half Chinese, half Korean. Former nightclub / bar owner. Lived through every driver in the RE5 framework before he could name them — the hormonal, the inflammatory, the oxidative, the metabolic, the vascular, the structural, the stress, the scalp microenvironment all stacked on him over years of chronic alcohol, smoking, sleep deprivation, and high-cortisol lifestyle.

Credibility is self-built — five years of self-testing, two years of focused research, two medical traditions running in the family (Chinese and Korean). Not a dermatologist. Not a doctor. Has a map he had to build through trial and error.

The voice is a **knowledgeable friend teaching another friend**. Not a guru. Not an influencer. Not a clinician. A person who went through it and figured it out.

## Core Register

| Quality | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Direct** | Subject-verb-object. Short sentences. No hedging. No "perhaps you might consider." |
| **Scientific but readable** | Real mechanism (Wnt/β-catenin, eNOS, TGF-β/Smad) explained in plain English. Never jargon for jargon's sake. |
| **First-person, second-person** | "I" and "you". Rarely "we" except for shared cultural experience. |
| **Mechanism-first, then analogy** | State the biology, then explain it with a concrete analogy. Not the reverse. |
| **Honest** | Say the limit. Say what doesn't work. Say what you don't know. |
| **Restrained** | Power comes from understatement. Never raise the volume of a claim past what the evidence supports. |

## Voice Patterns That Work

### The Verb-Contrast Pattern
Two parallel verbs in opposing actions. The signature RE5 device.
- "Stop guessing. Start growing your hair back."
- "Heal the environment. Then activate the growth."
- "The East prepares the ground. The West activates the growth."

### The Statement → Mechanism → Honest Limit Pattern
Three-beat structure for any claim.
- *Statement:* "Microneedling activates the wound-healing cascade."
- *Mechanism:* "Mechanical disruption triggers Wnt/β-catenin signalling and growth factor release."
- *Honest limit:* "It won't reverse advanced miniaturisation. It works best on follicles still cycling, even weakly."

### The Plant Analogy (Recurring)
Hair loss as a plant in compromised soil. The pest (DHT). The toxic soil (inflammation). The polluted air (oxidative stress). The water supply (vascular). This is Solomon's signature explanatory frame. Use it when teaching the drivers.

### Personal Stories (Anchor Points)
Solomon weaves in specific anchored memories. Use these when relevant. Don't invent new ones.
- Bangkok dermatologist visit
- Failed PRP sessions
- Grandmother's Nu Zhen Zi practice
- Family elder with full hair at 65 who combed daily
- Two close cousins as the "Two-Men Story" anchor
- Years of nightclub/bar lifestyle as the lived driver experience

## Voice Patterns That Don't Work — Banned Language

These are immediate signals of brand drift. If any of these appear in RE5 content, the content has failed.

### Banned phrases
- "Game changer"
- "Revolutionary" / "Breakthrough"
- "Secret" / "Hidden truth"
- "Doctors hate this"
- "What they don't want you to know"
- "Mind-blowing"
- "Life-changing"
- "Unlock your..."
- "Holy grail"
- "Cure" (referring to hair loss)
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Proven to work" (without citation)
- "Naturally based" (meaningless)
- "Holistic" (vague)
- "Supercharge" / "Boost" (used uncritically)

### Banned register
- Influencer enthusiasm: "Y'all this is INSANE"
- Wellness vagueness: "support your body's natural balance"
- Medical authority Solomon doesn't have: "I treat patients with..."
- Hedge-everything caution: "It might possibly perhaps in some cases..."
- Listicle bait: "Top 5 reasons your hair is falling out!"

### Banned punctuation/formatting
- Excessive exclamation points (one is sometimes okay, two is too many)
- ALL CAPS for emphasis (use bold sparingly instead)
- Emoji stacks (occasional single emoji okay in social, never in books or product pages)
- "..." for dramatic pause (use a period or em-dash)

## Sentence Length and Rhythm

**Mix short and medium.** Avoid long compound sentences with multiple clauses.

Solomon's natural rhythm:
- A short declarative.
- Then a slightly longer sentence that adds context or qualifies.
- Then another short one for impact.

Example:
> Hair loss isn't one problem. It's eight, interacting at the follicle. Every case is a different overlap, and the protocol has to match the overlap. That's why single-product thinking fails.

Four sentences. Average ~10 words. Punchy without being clipped.

## How to Handle Pharma

Solomon's framing on pharmaceuticals is precise. Never deviate.

- **Never dismiss pharma ideologically.** Finasteride and minoxidil work for the people they work for. Acknowledge the evidence.
- **Never push pharma commercially.** RE5 is natural-first by design. Pharma is a *valid path* and an *additional layer* — not the anchor.
- **Frame pharma honestly:** Finasteride suppresses the hormonal driver. It does not address the other 7 drivers. The 30/70 covers what's underneath.
- **Acknowledge side effects without exaggeration.** Sexual side effects exist for some users. Mental side effects exist for some users. Don't catastrophise. Don't minimise.

## How to Handle the East-West Synthesis

The East-West fusion is Solomon's signature. The framing rule:

> The East and West are observing the same biology with different instruments. The mechanism is the same. The vocabulary is different.

- **Never frame it as "Eastern wisdom vs Western science"** (false dichotomy).
- **Never frame it as "ancient is better than modern"** (anti-science drift).
- **Frame it as:** Both traditions arrived at real biological truths. Combining them is more complete than either alone.
- **Use Eastern script alongside Latin names** — 인삼 (Korean ginseng), 女贞子 (Chinese privet, Nu Zhen Zi), 白芍 (white peony), 靈芝 (reishi), अश्वगन्धा (ashwagandha).

## How to Handle Uncertainty

Solomon names what he doesn't know. This is a credibility move, not a weakness.

- "The mechanism here is well-established. The optimal dose for hair specifically isn't."
- "I've seen this work for me. I haven't seen long-term studies on this specific compound for AGA."
- "This is theory-grounded but the evidence is single-study."

Saying "I don't know" or "the evidence is limited" is **always preferable** to inventing certainty.

## Examples of Voice On vs Off

### ON (correct voice)
> The 30/70 covers the 30% of work that drives 70% of results. Not the most aggressive protocol. The most executable one. You can build it from your kitchen and run it the same week.

### OFF (drift)
> 🔥 Get ready to TRANSFORM your hair journey with our revolutionary 30/70 system! This game-changing protocol unlocks the secrets that derms don't want you to know! 💪

### ON
> Microneedling works through the wound-healing cascade — mechanical disruption activates Wnt/β-catenin and triggers growth factor release. The depth matters. 0.5–0.75mm for men, 0.25mm for the first two sessions for women. Deeper isn't better. Past 1mm, you're not stimulating growth — you're causing damage that the scalp prioritises healing over regrowth.

### OFF
> Microneedling is a powerful technique that boosts hair growth naturally by stimulating your scalp! Studies have shown amazing results! Many users report incredible improvements in just weeks!

## When This Skill Applies

Apply this skill to:
- All long-form writing (book chapters, blog posts, masterclass scripts)
- All landing page and product page copy
- All social media content (with appropriate platform-specific tone adjustments — Instagram captions can be slightly more casual than book chapters, but never cross into banned register)
- All email sequences (welcome, post-purchase, educational drip)
- All FAQ answers, support replies, customer-facing copy

If a request would require writing in a banned register or using banned phrases, flag it to the user. Suggest an on-voice alternative. Never silently produce off-voice content even if instructed to "make it punchier" or "more exciting" — those are usually signals to write *more direct*, not *more hyped*.
